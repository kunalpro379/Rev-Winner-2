Enhancement Title

Shift Gears 2.0 and Customer Query Pitches Advanced Negotiation and Objection Intelligence Layer

Only enhance internal reasoning layer of:
• Shift Gears
• Customer Query Pitches

All previous bug fixes requested earlier must remain intact.

No change to response speed outside these two modules.

Backward compatibility required.

OBJECTIVE

Enhance Shift Gears and Customer Query Pitches with a universal objection handling, technical response, strategic reframing and negotiation intelligence framework capable of handling:

• Price objections
• Technical objections
• Feature comparison
• Security concerns
• Compliance concerns
• Switching resistance
• Stakeholder alignment
• Budget constraints
• Contract negotiation
• Timing delays
• Competitive displacement
• Proof of value requests
• Procurement pressure
• Legal redlines
• Emotional disinterest
• Authority gaps
• Strategic misalignment

System must adapt based on context of live conversation.

CORE FRAMEWORK TO IMPLEMENT

Create internal reasoning engine structured as:

EMPATHY → DIAGNOSE → CLASSIFY → REFRAME → VALUE ELEVATE → RISK CONTRAST → NEGOTIATE → MICRO CLOSE

PHASE 1
REAL TIME OBJECTION CLASSIFICATION ENGINE

Shift Gears must detect objection type using transcript signals.

Build classification buckets:

Financial Objection

Technical Feasibility Objection

Security or Compliance Objection

Operational Complexity Objection

Switching Cost Objection

Political or Stakeholder Objection

Timing or Priority Objection

Competitive Comparison

Negotiation Pressure

Hidden Objection

Return internal tag before generating response.

PHASE 2
RESPONSE CONSTRUCTION FRAME

For every objection type, follow this structure:

Step 1 Tactical Empathy
Acknowledge without agreeing against product

Step 2 Clarifying Diagnostic Question
Ask 1 precision question to uncover real concern

Step 3 Strategic Reframe
Shift lens from surface concern to business impact

Step 4 Loss Amplification
Quantify potential downside of inaction

Step 5 Value Anchor
Tie product capability to business outcome

Step 6 Controlled Next Step
Offer low friction advancement

PHASE 3
TECHNICAL SCENARIO HANDLING

When objection is technical:

Use Technical Resolution Layer:

Clarify environment

Confirm architecture constraints

Identify integration layer

Highlight compatibility

Compare against industry benchmark

Offer sandbox or proof test

Avoid generic marketing language.
Use technical clarity and structure.

PHASE 4
NON TECHNICAL SCENARIO HANDLING

When objection is emotional or vague:

Use Insight Bridge Model:

Normalize concern

Convert abstract fear into measurable risk

Attach financial impact

Attach reputational impact

Attach operational impact

PHASE 5
NEGOTIATION FRAMEWORK

Add Negotiation Intelligence Module inside Shift Gears.

When pricing pressure or contract negotiation detected:

Activate Structured Negotiation Model:

Step 1 Context Extraction
Detect if pressure is budget real or leverage tactic

Step 2 Trade Value Not Price
If discount requested, require give back:

• Longer term
• Volume commitment
• Case study
• Faster close
• Multi module expansion

Step 3 Conditional Framing
Instead of reducing price say:

If we structure X commitment, we can explore Y flexibility.

Step 4 Anchor to Outcome
Reframe negotiation around ROI not cost

Step 5 Protect Margin Guardrails
System must not suggest arbitrary discount
Only suggest structured value exchange

Step 6 Escalation Logic
If beyond seller authority, suggest executive alignment

PHASE 6
COMPETITIVE DEFENSE MODEL

When competitor mentioned:

Do not attack competitor

Clarify evaluation criteria

Reframe around differentiation

Introduce risk contrast

Offer side by side proof

Suggest limited pilot

PHASE 7
AUTHORITY GAP HANDLING

If buyer says need to check with team:

Map decision hierarchy

Ask about evaluation criteria

Offer to equip champion

Provide summary ammunition

Secure follow up

PHASE 8
MICRO COMMITMENT ENGINE

Every output must end with one of:

• Clarifying question
• Pilot suggestion
• Stakeholder alignment step
• Technical validation step
• Timeline agreement

Never end with generic closing statement.
