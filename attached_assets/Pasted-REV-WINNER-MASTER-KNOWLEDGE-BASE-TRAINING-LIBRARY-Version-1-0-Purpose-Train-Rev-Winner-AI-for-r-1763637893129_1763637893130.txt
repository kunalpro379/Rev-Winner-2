🔥 SECTION 3 — PLAYBOOKS (SPIN, MEDDIC, CHALLENGER, BANT, GAP)

This helps Rev Winner deliver hyper-contextual prompts based on how the buyer speaks.

⭐ A. SPIN Selling Playbook
Situation Questions

“Walk me through how your current process works.”

“Who on your team is most impacted by inefficiencies?”

“How do reps currently prepare for calls?”

Problem Questions

“Where do deals typically break down today?”

“Where do reps hesitate during live conversations?”

“What frustrates you about your current sales tools?”

Implication Questions

“What does missing key buying signals do to your forecast accuracy?”

“How much revenue slips because reps don’t know what to say next?”

Need-Payoff Questions

“What would real-time assistance do for your team’s performance?”

“How would your win rate be affected if every rep behaved like your top rep?”

⭐ B. MEDDIC Playbook

Metrics:
“What measurable improvement are you expecting this quarter?”

Economic Buyer:
“Who signs off when you add new sales tools?”

Decision Criteria:
“What matters more — speed, coaching, accuracy, or consistency?”

Decision Process:
“How do you evaluate solutions like this internally?”

Identify Pain:
“What’s the number one reason deals don’t close?”

Champion:
“Who inside your org would be the biggest supporter of this?”

⭐ C. Challenger Playbook

Teach Rev Winner to challenge respectfully:

“I want to share an insight most leaders miss…”

“There’s a hidden cost that many teams overlook…”

“Your competitors have already solved this problem — here’s how.”

⭐ D. BANT Playbook

Budget:
“What’s the cost of one lost deal compared to a tool like this?”

Authority:
“Besides you, who else is involved in this decision?”

Need:
“What exactly do your reps struggle with during calls?”

Timeline:
“When are you aiming to improve rep performance?”

⭐ E. GAP Selling Playbook

“Where are you today and where do you want to be?”

“What’s the gap in skill, process, or tools that blocks you?”

“What happens if the gap stays unaddressed?”
