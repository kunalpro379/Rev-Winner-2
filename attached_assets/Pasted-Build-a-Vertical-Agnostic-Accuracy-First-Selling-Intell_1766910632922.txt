Build a Vertical-Agnostic Accuracy-First Selling Intelligence Engine for Rev Winner. This is a modifcation needed in Present to Win Section. 
that assists sellers across ALL industries, products, and services.

Rev Winner must support:
- Technology & SaaS sales
- Financial products & services
- Consulting, implementation, and professional services
- Managed services
- Healthcare, education, logistics, manufacturing
- Any B2B or B2C selling context

CORE OBJECTIVE:
Enable sellers to communicate clear, credible, and accurate differentiation
without exaggeration, misinformation, or industry-specific compliance risk.

--------------------------------------------------
1. DOMAIN-AWARE CONTEXT DETECTION
--------------------------------------------------
Before generating any content, Rev Winner must:
- Identify the selling domain (tech, finance, services, healthcare, etc.)
- Detect deal type (product, subscription, service, advisory, outcome-based)
- Adjust tone, claims, and compliance sensitivity accordingly

If domain cannot be confidently detected:
→ Default to conservative, neutral positioning language

--------------------------------------------------
2. SOURCE-OF-TRUTH HIERARCHY (MANDATORY)
--------------------------------------------------
All generated insights must follow this priority order:

1) Customer-provided "Train Me" data
   (documents, recordings, pricing sheets, policies, proposals)

2) Official, publicly available sources
   (vendor websites, brochures, regulatory disclosures)

3) Industry-standard practices and norms
   (used cautiously, never as absolute facts)

4) General selling principles
   (used only when domain-specific data is unavailable)

If information is uncertain:
→ Clearly label as "Positioning Insight" or "Typical Market Observation"

--------------------------------------------------
3. CLAIM SAFETY & CLASSIFICATION
--------------------------------------------------
Every generated statement must be internally classified as one of:

- Verified Fact
- Positioning Advantage
- Use-Case Strength
- Typical Market Practice
- Trade-Off / Consideration
- Opinion (Seller Framing)

Disallowed across all verticals:
- Absolute claims (best, only, guaranteed, fastest, cheapest)
- Unverified performance numbers
- Regulatory or financial guarantees
- Medical, legal, or financial advice language

--------------------------------------------------
4. DIFFERENTIATION FRAMEWORK (UNIVERSAL)
--------------------------------------------------
Differentiation must be framed using one or more of:

- Problem-fit (what problem this is designed for)
- Approach & methodology
- Delivery model & effort required
- Risk profile & predictability
- Customization vs standardization
- Time-to-value
- Cost transparency
- Trust & accountability

NOT:
- Feature checklists alone
- Aggressive competitor takedowns
- Binary yes/no superiority tables

--------------------------------------------------
5. COMPETITOR & ALTERNATIVE HANDLING
--------------------------------------------------
When alternatives exist (competitors, in-house teams, status quo):

- Acknowledge strengths of alternatives
- Clearly state scenarios where alternatives are a better fit
- Position Rev Winner's supported offering honestly

Example:
"Option A works well when [condition].
This approach is more suitable when [different condition]."

--------------------------------------------------
6. BATTLE CARD GENERATION RULES
--------------------------------------------------
Each Battle Card must include:

- Buyer Context & Intent
- Common Buyer Concerns
- Differentiation Talking Points (safe & accurate)
- When This Offering Fits Well
- When This Offering May Not Fit
- Objection Handling (fact-based, calm)
- Language to Avoid (risk phrases)

--------------------------------------------------
7. PITCH DECK GENERATION RULES
--------------------------------------------------
Pitch decks must:

- Adapt structure by vertical
- Emphasize outcomes over features
- Avoid misleading comparisons
- Use scenario-based storytelling
- Include disclaimers where required (finance, healthcare, legal)

--------------------------------------------------
8. CASE STUDY GENERATION RULES
--------------------------------------------------
Case studies must be explicitly tagged as:

- Real (verified)
- Anonymized (real but identity hidden)
- Illustrative (hypothetical example)

Rules:
- No fabricated customer names or logos
- No invented metrics
- Focus on problem → approach → outcome
- Avoid exaggerated ROI claims

--------------------------------------------------
9. LIVE-CONVERSATION RESPONSE BEHAVIOR
--------------------------------------------------
During live sales conversations:

- Prefer trust preservation over persuasion
- Use probabilistic language where needed
- If confidence is low, respond with:
  “In similar situations, sellers typically see…”
  “Based on available information…”

--------------------------------------------------
10. COMPLIANCE-AWARE ADAPTATION
--------------------------------------------------
For regulated industries (finance, healthcare, legal):

- Automatically soften claims
- Avoid advice-giving language
- Encourage consultation with licensed professionals
- Surface disclaimers silently (non-intrusive)

--------------------------------------------------
11. AUDITABILITY & TRACEABILITY
--------------------------------------------------
For every generated insight, log:
- Source category used
- Confidence level (High / Medium / Low)
- Domain detected
- Timestamp of generation

--------------------------------------------------
END GOAL:
Rev Winner should act as a universal selling co-pilot
that increases seller confidence, protects credibility,
and improves buyer trust across every industry.
